# 🏥 Afya CHW AI

Swahili-language AI decision-support tool for Community Health Workers — embeds Kenya MOH triage protocols for the 105,000+ CHWs operating across Kenya counties.

Embedded clinical protocols (Kenya MOH + WHO) in Swahili. Works in low-bandwidth environments.
Danger sign detection → referral triggers → immediate action → follow-up guidance.

## Research Basis
- **arXiv:2408.17216** — "Democratizing AI in Africa: Federated Learning for Low-Resource Edge Devices" (2024). Demonstrates AI viability on Raspberry Pi-class hardware across 5 African countries for health applications.
- **"Edge Intelligence Unleashed" (2025)** — Journal of Edge Computing survey of LLM deployment in resource-constrained environments.
- **arXiv:2601.09716** — "Opportunities and Challenges of NLP for African Languages" (2026). Swahili is identified as a language where AI tools can have high impact due to large speaker population and low existing tool coverage.
- **WHO ANC Reference** — "WHO Recommendations on Antenatal Care for a Positive Pregnancy Experience" (2016) — 8-contact model.
- **Kenya MOH CHEW Curriculum** — Community Health Extension Worker training materials.

## Coverage
- Fever/Malaria assessment
- Respiratory infection (cough, IMCI pneumonia)
- Diarrhoea/ORS/Zinc protocol
- Antenatal care danger signs
- Acute malnutrition (MUAC screening)

## Why This Matters
Kenya has 105,000 Community Health Workers. Average patient-to-CHW ratio: 3,000:1 in rural areas.
CHWs operate far from facilities with no real-time clinical support.
This tool gives them AI-backed protocol guidance in Swahili, offline-capable.
